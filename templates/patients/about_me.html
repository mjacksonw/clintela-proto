Who you are

What should we call you?

A nickname, your first name, whatever feels like you. We'll use this when we check in.

Tell us about yourself

There's no right answer. Just anything that helps us understand you as a person, not just a patient.

Some people write things like "Retired teacher, love my book club, live in a second-floor walk-up" or "Dad of three, work from home, my dog is my best nurse."

Who's in your corner?

The people who are helping you through this — family, friends, neighbors. Knowing who's around you helps us help all of you.

e.g. "My daughter Sarah checks on me daily," "Neighbor walks my dog while I recover"

Your daily life

Your living situation

This helps us understand your day-to-day — things like stairs, having help at home, or being on your own.

Your daily routines

Helps us time check-ins around your day — not at 6 AM if you're a night owl, not during your favorite show.

e.g. "Morning person — up by 6:30. Tea and crossword before anything else."

Your recovery

What are you looking forward to?

What do you want to get back to? A goal gives your recovery direction — and gives us something to cheer you toward.

e.g. "Getting back to my garden," "Walking my daughter down the aisle in June"

Is anything on your mind?

Worries are completely normal after surgery. Naming them helps us keep an eye on the things that matter to you.

e.g. "Stairs at home," "Managing all these medications," "Being alone at night"

What matters most to you?

Everyone's different. Some people value independence above all. Others want to understand everything. Some just want to feel safe. What's yours?

How we connect

How do you like to hear things?

Some people like a chatty, warm tone. Others prefer we get to the point. We'll match your style.

When's a good time to check in?

We'll try to reach out when it's convenient — not when you're resting or busy.

Language or cultural preferences

Anything we should know to make you feel more at home — a language you prefer, cultural or religious considerations, dietary needs we should keep in mind.

You can update any of this anytime. Nothing is set in stone.
